Blender learning

Nothing out there in 3D is dirt simple.

Just break your learning tasks into chunks.

  • Textures
  • Modeling
  • Rendering
  • Lighting
  • Animation
  • Sculpting
  • NLA
  1. I’d suggest starting with texture tutorials so you can learn how to apply pbr textures (there are a lot of free pbr textures). At first, stick with cycles mode, and get familiar with how textures interact with environmental lighting. This will be important versus how things look using EEVEE.
  2. Then start modeling a few simple things, and apply your downloaded textures.
  3. rendering. Learn about samples. Learn what cycles mode is, and how it compares to EEVEE. Your textures have to be set up differently for taking advantage of EEVEE’s speed. This will branch into learning about lighting and shadows and how they work compared to cycles
  4. Then learn some 3-point lighting techniques. This will teach you things to have your models looking decent.
  5. Then learn to rig up an armature for animation. Doesn’t have to be complex. This will spawn into learning about weight mapping. Weight mapping will open doors into dynamic painting and other effects for later.
  6. Your sculpting can come at any time during modeling, but you should pursue learning to model a general shape and add later details. This will lead to retopo modeling or modeling for better topology, or polygon/edge flow. Learning to remesh may be appropriate at this stage.
  7. NLA non-linear animation. Building an animation up from smaller poses that can be saved as simple actions and combined for later use.

Blender has the biggest toolset for learning that is out there right now. It can be daunting, but not if you break up your learning tasks into a syllabus of sorts
