Learning Milestones Link

Learning Milestones

Working in the Web Design business has taught me a lot of things. This page gives me a chance to display a list of milestones to keep me on track, as well as a place to remind myself of attainable career goals that have been set. This is an ever-changing list, some items may not be current.

Scripting:
Good to get a feel for more powerful computer languages and learn about structuring input for real-world functionality.

ActionScript:

AS1. Learned! But not very used. Completed two Flash books at the time— one covering the IDE, and one completely about advanced AS1— before online examples started withering, and changing (undiscernably for me) into AS2. I had to go it alone, with Google and some forums.

Objects: Using the prototype functionality, I understood the structure of classes, but struggled with scope at the time.

Scope: The THIS keyword. Wow, this was what EVERY SITE said that people struggle with. Yep. Me too.

AS2. I decided that I would take a class to get familiar with the new language, but I got in a little over my head. The book that I had finished was partially entitled Advanced Training From The Soure, so I thought that a local weekend class was just the ticket. Since with the aquisition of Macromedia, the actionscript language was changed. Restructuring of the language allowing the IDE compiler to catch more errors. And as it evolved, some of my bad coding habits had to be unlearned. Boy, howdy. I'm still unlearning my bad coding habits!

Progress is slowing for me since more examples of AS2 are disappearing every day. Thank God for forums, or I would be completely in the dark. I hope to be over AS2 in the next few months.

Classes: Strong-typing was nothing compared to this. I've read Essential Action Script 2, with a focus on OOP, so I have good foundation. Right now (End of January) I just can't figure out how to manipulate class properties outside of a class.

AS3: I hope to be using it by May, 2009. (This may include Flex, too.) OOP structure won't necessarily be a problem, only Flash's dependancy on it may be at first. Although I should be able to pick it up quickly with XML and other enhancements I've had a chance to see. I have a few books on this that may help. I hope to be rather fluent with it and using classes dynamically before then.

JavaScript:

Well, I'm just a beginner in JS. I can toggle CSS classes and styles on the page, and I can build crude functions. No methods or classes for me yet (although the Essential AS2 book details classes in Java to be similar to ActionScripts. Or was that similarity to JavaSCRIPT? There is a difference, I've been told.)

REAL PROGRAMMING/ Interface design

With scripting out of the way, that will pave the way for getting my hands dirty with a more structured endeavor into C#, using a clean, XHTML-compliant (unlike this site) wrapper.

Visual Studio:

At the last company I worked for, I was given a chance to play with a website inside this beast. I was given some instruction on how to manipulate the website, maintaining page controls and master page functionality. So while the lead dev was on vacation, I put the company's website online, copying and pasting the appropriate parts like a good web monkey. That was all good, but I hope to be creating dynamic web pages in VS '08 by the middle of next week.

Page Four: The Belle Air was a superb restaurant, owned by my friend's brother. We hung out here while in Greece, and were able to enjoy lots of great food with a local flair.

Easter was such a wonderful time in Greece; much different than western Easter celebrations that have more to do with the Easter bunny than the real reason for the holiday. Even though Greek Orthodox members have a version of the "Easter Egg," the egg symbol over here has more biblical symbolism than back in the states. They are died red usually, as a reminder of Christ's sacrifice.

On Good Friday, starting with extinguishing all the lights in the cathedral, Greek Orthodox people carry a beautiful representation of Christ through the streets to remind everyone of His spiritual journey before His ultimate sacrifice. Then, with a fervent excitement, they return It to their cathedral and light all the candles and lights that had been extinguished. It was a beautiful sight! Too bad we didn't get to stay for the whole Easter celebration. They have fireworks to mark the occasion.

Joseph Henderson Online Portfolio

Hello. My name is Joseph Henderson. Digital imagery has been my main focus since college, although my background is in traditional design and illustration. Creating quality artwork is my passion.

My first digital image was an attempt at a duplication of a Roger Rabbit poster on a friend's TRS80, all in an IBM clone's 16 color glory.

Then, I got an Amiga500. Oh, that was the stuff. I was creating everything I could think of with my mouse.

As I saw computer graphics getting better, I wanted to create art with them, not just play games.

So, I tried making art with any program-- on any computer-- I could access. A broad spectrum of work developed by using numerous software applications on various platforms.

So this site showcases my broad capacity for different styles and techniques, with big sample of the history of my work produced from high school and during college, as well as recent work.
